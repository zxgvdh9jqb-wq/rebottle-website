üëï ReWear Collection Drive - February 2025

In February 2025, MCCRI successfully launched the ReWear Collection Drive, a month-long initiative focused on collecting and repurposing old clothing items. This programme aimed to reduce textile waste while providing materials for fashion upcycling workshops and community textile art projects.

üìÖ Event Duration: February 1-28, 2025
üì¶ Items Collected: 3,847 pieces of clothing
üë• Total Participants: 284 individuals from across Greater Manchester
üè¢ Partner Organizations: 28 schools, universities, and companies

Participants from schools, universities, and businesses across the region contributed unwanted clothing items including shirts, trousers, dresses, jackets, and accessories. These items were sorted, with wearable pieces donated to local charities and the remainder transformed into textile art, quilts, bags, and other creative projects.

The ReWear Collection Drive demonstrated the power of community collaboration in addressing textile waste, one of the fastest-growing environmental challenges. We are incredibly proud of everyone who participated!

‚ôªÔ∏è ReBottle Art Project Overview

The ReBottle Art Project is our flagship annual initiative that brings together creativity, community, and environmental consciousness. This year's project runs from November 6, 2025 to December 6, 2025.

üéØ Mission Statement: To encourage the collection and artistic reuse of glass bottles, transforming everyday waste into extraordinary art while raising awareness about recycling and sustainability.

Participants from all backgrounds are invited to gather glass bottles of all shapes, sizes, and colours. These bottles are then cleaned, sorted by our dedicated team, and transformed into stunning artworks including large-scale installations, intricate mosaics, decorative lamps, garden sculptures, and functional art pieces.

Each year, the project attracts over 600 participants from across Greater Manchester and beyond. The journey from collection to creation is collaborative, educational, and deeply rewarding.

Phase 1: Collection (Nov 6-20)

Community members collect and submit glass bottles to our designated collection points across Manchester.

Phase 2: Sorting & Planning (Nov 21-25)

Our team sorts bottles by colour, size, and condition. Artists begin conceptualizing their projects.

Phase 3: Creation (Nov 26-Dec 4)

Workshops and creation sessions where participants work alongside professional artists to build artworks.

Phase 4: Exhibition (Dec 5-6)

The grand finale! All artworks are displayed at the annual "Manchester Green Creativity Exhibition" at Manchester Central.
