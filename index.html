About MCCRI

The Manchester Community Creative Reuse Initiative (MCCRI) is a registered cultural and environmental organisation dedicated to promoting sustainability and creative reuse of materials. Founded in 2014 in Manchester's Northern Quarter, MCCRI works with schools, universities, community centers, and artists to turn recyclable waste into impactful public artworks.

Our programmes have been supported by the Greater Manchester Green Fund, UK Youth Environmental Alliance, and local businesses. MCCRI hosts workshops, exhibitions, and training sessions that combine environmental education with hands-on creativity.

ReBottle Art Project Overview

The ReBottle Art Project is MCCRI's flagship programme. Its mission is to encourage the collection and reuse of glass bottles for artistic purposes. Participants from across Manchester gather to collect bottles of all shapes, sizes, and colours. These bottles are then cleaned, sorted, and transformed into a wide range of artworks including installations, mosaics, lamps, and decorative pieces.

Each year, the project attracts over 600 participants, including students, young designers, and environmentally conscious citizens. The final artworks are displayed at the annual "Manchester Green Creativity Exhibition", drawing visitors from the community and media coverage from local outlets.

Programme Structure

The ReBottle Art Project runs in several structured phases:

  • Bottle Collection Week: Participants gather glass bottles from approved sources and local communities.
  • Cleaning & Sorting: Volunteers prepare bottles for artistic use.
  • Workshops: Led by professional artists, participants learn techniques such as glass-cutting, mosaic assembly, lamp-making, and safe tool usage.
  • Installation Building: Groups collaborate to create large-scale installations for public display.
  • Final Exhibition: The artworks are presented at Manchester Creative Hall with press coverage and public attendance.

Reward Scheme

To incentivize participation, MCCRI offers a £5 Environmental Contribution Grant for each participant submitting at least 10 recyclable glass bottles. This grant is intended to support workshop participation and encourage further environmental action.

Important: Due to limited funding, each participant may only claim this grant once per year.

Our Mission & Vision

MCCRI is committed to promoting sustainable practices through artistic innovation. We aim to educate youth on environmental responsibility while encouraging creativity and community engagement. Our long-term vision includes expanding workshops across the UK and developing a national recycled-art exhibition network.
